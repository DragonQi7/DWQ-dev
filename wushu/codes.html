手型 Hand Forms
拳 Quán — Fist

Hand Form Deduction Code 01 手型 Hand Forms

Applies to: Changquan · Jianshu · Daoshu · Qiangshu · Gunshu

● red = fault zones   □ green = correct

1

Face of Fist Uneven

拳面不平

The striking surface of the fist must be flat — all four knuckles aligned. An uneven fist face (one knuckle protruding) is a deduction.

2

Thumb Not Pressing on Index & Middle Finger

拇指未压在食指和中指第二节上

The thumb must wrap around and press firmly on the second segment (middle phalanx) of both the index and middle fingers. A loose or misplaced thumb is a fault.

Deduction: 0.1 per occurrence. If both faults appear in one technique, only 0.1 is deducted once for that technique.
掌 Zhǎng — Palm

Hand Form Deduction Code 02 手型 Hand Forms

Applies to: Changquan · Jianshu · Daoshu · Qiangshu · Gunshu

● red = fault zones

1

Four Fingers Not Straight and Held Together

四指未伸直并拢

All four fingers must be fully extended and pressed tightly together with no gaps. Bent or splayed fingers are a fault.

2

Thumb Not Bent and Held Tightly

拇指未弯曲紧扣于虎口处

The thumb must be bent inward and pressed firmly into the "tiger's mouth" (虎口) — the web between thumb and index finger. A loose or straight thumb is a deduction.

Deduction: 0.1 per occurrence. If both faults appear in one technique, only 0.1 is deducted once for that technique.
勾手 Gōu Shǒu — Hook Hand

Hand Form Deduction Code 03 手型 Hand Forms

Applies to: Changquan · Jianshu · Daoshu · Qiangshu · Gunshu

● red = fault zones

1

Five Fingers Not Pinched Together

五指未拢拢

All five fingertips must come together to a single point, like a beak. If any fingers are splayed or separated, it's a fault.

2

Wrist Not Hooked Completely

腕未屈

The wrist must be fully flexed (hooked downward) so the pinched fingertips point toward the ground. An incomplete hook is a deduction.

Deduction: 0.1 per occurrence. If both faults appear in one technique, only 0.1 is deducted once for that technique.
剑指 Jiàn Zhǐ — Sword Fingers

Hand Form Deduction Code 04 手型 Hand Forms

Applies to: Changquan · Jianshu · Daoshu · Qiangshu · Gunshu

● red = fault zones

1

Index & Middle Fingers Not Straight and Together

食指与中指未伸直并拢

The index and middle fingers must be fully extended and pressed tightly together, forming the "sword blade." Bent or separated fingers are a fault.

2

Thumb Not Pressing on Ring & Little Finger

拇指未压在无名指与小指上

The thumb must curl in and press firmly on the ring and little fingers (which are themselves curled in). A loose or misplaced thumb is a deduction.

Deduction: 0.1 per occurrence. If both faults appear in one technique, only 0.1 is deducted once for that technique.

Gazing at the Moon Balance Code 16

Torso horizontal, waist twisted to look back over supporting leg

- - - green = horizontal reference
● red = deduction fault zones

1

Torso 45° or More Above Horizontal

躯干高于水平45°或超过45°

The torso must be kept low — no more than 45° above horizontal. Standing too upright is a deduction.

2

Waist Not Twisted to Look Back

The waist must twist toward the supporting-leg side so the athlete gazes to the rear. Failing to twist counts as a fault.

3

屈收腿脚背未绷平

The instep (top of foot) of the raised bent leg must be pointed and extended flat. A relaxed or flexed foot is a deduction.

Low spinning sweep with supporting leg crouched

- - - green = horizontal reference
● red = deduction fault zones

1

Supporting Thigh Above Horizontal

支撑腿大腿高于水平

The supporting leg must be crouched low enough that the thigh is at or below horizontal. Rising too high is a fault.

2

Sweeping Foot Leaves the Ground

扫转腿脚掌在扫转时触地后离地

The sole of the sweeping foot must maintain contact with the ground throughout the sweep. Lifting off the floor after initial contact is a deduction.

3

扫转腿弯曲45°或超过45°

The sweeping leg must remain extended. Bending the knee 45° or more during the sweep is a deduction.

Sweeping Foot Leaves the Ground

2

Sweeping Leg Bent 45° or More

扫转腿弯曲45°或超过45°

The sweeping leg must remain extended and as straight as possible. A bend of 45° or more at the knee is a fault.

弹腿 · 蹬腿 · 踹腿 — Snap Kick · Heel Push Kick · Side Kick

Leg Technique Deduction Code 23 腿法 Leg Techniques

Applies to: Changquan · Jianshu · Daoshu · Qiangshu · Gunshu

Dashed = bent start position · Solid = extended final position

1

Kicking Leg Does Not Show Obvious Bend-to-Straight Transition

弹（蹬、踹）腿由屈到伸摆动小于45°

The kicking leg must start from a clearly bent position (45° or more) and extend to completely straight. If the range of motion from bent to straight is less than 45°, it's a deduction — the kick looks like a "push" instead of a snap.

Applies to all three kicks: Snap Kick (弹腿), Heel Push Kick (蹬腿), and Side Kick (踹腿). The same "bend-to-straight" criterion applies to each.
正踢腿 · 侧踢腿 — Front Stretch Kick · Side Stretch Kick

Leg Technique Deduction Code 24 腿法 Leg Techniques

Applies to: Changquan · Jianshu · Daoshu · Qiangshu · Gunshu

● red = fault zones   - - - green = correct

1

Knee Bent at Apex of the Kick

膝关节弯曲

At the highest point of the kick, the kicking leg must be completely straight — no bend at the knee whatsoever.

2

Heel of Supporting Leg Off the Floor

支撑腿脚跟离地

The standing leg's heel must stay firmly on the ground throughout the kick. Rising onto the ball of the foot is a deduction.

Deduction: 0.1 per occurrence. Applies to both Front Stretch Kick (正踢腿) and Side Stretch Kick (侧踢腿).
里合拍脚 · 摆莲拍脚 · 单拍脚 — Inward / Lotus / Front Slap Kick

Leg Technique Deduction Code 25 腿法 Leg Techniques

Applies to: Changquan · Jianshu · Daoshu · Qiangshu · Gunshu

● gold = slap contact   ● red = faults

1

Slapped Foot Not Above Shoulder Height

击响腿脚尖未过肩

The toes of the kicking foot must reach above shoulder level before the slap. If the foot is below the shoulder line, it's a deduction.

2

Slap Missed and/or Inaudible

未击响

The hand must make clear, audible contact with the foot. A missed slap or a slap that cannot be heard by the judges is a fault.

Applies to: Inward Slap Kick (里合拍脚), Lotus Slap Kick (摆莲拍脚), and Front Slap Kick (单拍脚). Both faults use the same Code 25.
提膝（独立）Tí Xī Dú Lì — Single Knee Raised Position

Leg Technique Deduction Code 26 腿法 Leg Techniques

Applies to: Changquan · Jianshu · Daoshu · Qiangshu · Gunshu

● red = fault zones   - - - green = correct

1

Raised Knee Not Above Waist Level

提膝未过腰

The knee of the raised leg must be lifted above waist height. A low knee position is a deduction.

2

Shank/Calf Not Turning Obliquely Inwards

提起腿小腿未斜垂里扣

The lower leg (shank) must hang downward and angle obliquely inward, not straight down or outward.

3

Foot Toes Not Pointed and Hooking Inwards

提起腿脚未崩平内收

The toes of the raised foot must be pointed (extended) and turned/hooked inward. Relaxed or outward-pointing toes are a fault.

Deduction: 0.1 per occurrence. All three faults share Code 26 — judges record just one deduction per technique even if multiple faults occur.

腾空飞脚 · 旋风脚 · 腾空摆莲 — Jumping Aerial Slap Kicks

Jumping Technique Deduction Code 30 跳跃 Jumping

Applies to: Changquan · Jianshu · Daoshu · Qiangshu · Gunshu

● gold = slap contact   ● red = faults

1

Toes of Slapped Foot Not Above Shoulder Level

击响腿脚尖未过肩

While airborne, the kicking foot must reach above shoulder height before the slap occurs. Below-shoulder kicks are a deduction.

2

Slap Missed and/or Inaudible

未击响

The hand must make clear, audible contact with the foot at the top of the kick. A whiff or quiet slap is a fault.

Applies to 5 techniques: Jumping Front Slap Kick (腾空飞脚), Jumping Slant Kick (腾空斜飞脚), Jumping Double Front Slap Kick (腾空双飞脚), Tornado Kick (旋风脚), and Jumping Lotus Kick (腾空摆莲).
腾空正踢腿 Téng Kōng Zhèng Tī Tuǐ — Jumping Front Straight Kick

Jumping Technique Deduction Code 31 跳跃 Jumping

Applies to: Changquan · Jianshu · Daoshu · Qiangshu · Gunshu

● red = fault zones   - - - green = correct

1

Hanging Leg Bent at Moment of Kick

踢腿脚触及或超过前额瞬间悬垂腿弯曲

At the exact moment the kicking foot's toes touch or pass the forehead, the hanging (non-kicking) leg must be completely straight. Any bend in the hanging leg is a deduction.

2

Torso-to-Hanging-Leg Angle Less Than 135°

踢腿脚触及或超过前额瞬间躯干与悬垂腿夹角小于135°

At the moment the kicking toes reach the forehead, the angle between the torso and the hanging leg must be at least 135°. If the body "tucks" too far forward (angle < 135°), it's a fault — the torso must remain relatively upright.

Key timing: Both faults are judged at one specific instant — the moment the kicking foot's toes touch or pass the forehead. Everything else about the jump is irrelevant to Code 31.

Spinning horizontal aerial with body near horizontal

- - - green = horizontal plane

Leg/s Bent 45° or More in the Air

腾空箭弹 Téng Kōng Jiàn Tán / 腾空蹬腿 Téng Kōng Dèng Tuǐ

Jumping Snap Kick · Jumping Heel Push Kick Code 34

Airborne snapping/pushing kick extending from bent to straight

- - - green = horizontal plane
● red = deduction fault zones

1

Kick Does Not Transit From Bent to Straight

弹（蹬）腿由屈到伸摆动小于45°

The snapping/pushing leg must show a clear transition from a bent position (45° or more) to fully straight. A lazy, barely extending kick is a fault.

2

Kick Below Horizontal Level

弹（蹬）出腿低于水平

The kicking leg must reach at least horizontal level at full extension. A kick that stays below the horizontal plane is a deduction.

步型 Stances & Footwork
弓步 Gōng Bù — Bow Stance

Technique Deduction Reference Code 50

Applies to: Changquan · Jianshu · Daoshu · Qiangshu · Gunshu

- - - green = correct reference
● red = deduction fault zones

1

Front Knee Not Above Instep

前膝膝部未达脚背

The front knee must come forward far enough to be directly above (or past) the instep of the front foot.

2

Front Thigh Not Parallel to the Ground

前腿大腿未达水平

The thigh of the bending (front) leg must be fully horizontal — parallel to the ground.

3

Rear Foot Sole Off the Floor

后腿脚掌任一部位明显离地

The entire sole of the back foot must stay flat on the ground. Lifting even slightly counts as a fault.

4

Rear Foot Not Hooked Inward

后腿脚尖未内扣

The back foot must angle obliquely inward toward the front — toes should face partly forward.

马步 Mǎ Bù — Horse Stance

Technique Deduction Reference Code 51

Symmetrical wide squat with thighs horizontal

- - - green = correct reference
● red = deduction fault zones

1

Thighs Not Horizontal

大腿未达水平

Both thighs must be parallel to the ground. A stance that is too high (thighs angled upward) is a deduction.

2

Feet Narrower Than Shoulder Width

两脚内侧间距小于本人肩宽

The inner edges of the feet must be at least shoulder-width apart. A narrow stance is a fault.

3

Knee/s Buckling Inwards

膝内跪

The knees must track outward over the feet. Caving the knees inward is a deduction.

4

Heel/s Raised Off the Ground

脚跟离地

Both heels must stay fully planted on the floor. Lifting either heel is a fault.

5

Toes Pointing Outward 45° or More

脚尖外展45°或超过45°

The toes of each foot should not splay outward 45° or more from forward.

握剑 Wò Jiàn — Gripping the Straight Sword

Sword Grip Technique Code 61

Fingers must not touch the blade edge above the guard

○ green = correct grip zone
● red = forbidden zone

1

Finger Touches the Blade Edge

手指触及剑刃

No finger should wrap around the top of the hand guard and touch the edge of the blade. The grip must remain entirely on or below the guard.

Key point: This is a safety and technique issue. In competition, any grip where a finger creeps above the guard onto the blade — even momentarily — triggers Code 61.

拦枪 Lán Qiāng / 拿枪 Ná Qiāng / 扎枪 Zhā Qiāng

Spear Tip Not Tracing a Clear Arc

拦、拿枪枪尖未明显划弧

During a horizontal thrust, the thrusting arm and the spear shaft must form one continuous straight horizontal line. Any bend or angle is a deduction.

Weapon Throwing & Catching Code 66

Toss and catch technique for all long and short weapons

○ green = correct single-hand catch
● red = deduction fault zones

1

Weapon Caught in a Hugging Manner

腾空盘腿360°侧扑 Téng Kōng Pán Tuǐ 360° Cè Pū

Other Errors — Definitions

  • Sway (70): Athlete’s upper body is displaced in 2 different directions — e.g., leans left/right, forward/backward, or circular — while attempting to maintain balance.
  • Shuffle (70): While standing or landing on one or both feet, any supporting foot moves or is displaced laterally.
  • Skip (70): While standing or landing on one or both feet, any supporting foot leaves the carpet in a skip or hop.
  • Additional Support (71): Athlete loses balance and uses a hand, elbow, knee, non-supporting leg, or the weapon as additional support. If the weapon hits the floor without force applied as support, it is a weapon-touching-floor deduction, not additional support.
  • Fall (72): Athlete contacts the floor with both hands, head, upper arm (above elbow), shoulder, torso, or buttocks — or any two or more body parts simultaneously — or one body part and the weapon. Intentional falls in Duilian are not deducted.
  • Weapon Deformed (73): Weapon deformed in excess of 45° from its original intended shape.
  • Out-of-Bounds (78): Any part of the athlete’s body touches the floor outside the boundary line. Weapon touching the floor outside the boundary, or body part extended past the boundary without floor contact, does not count.
  • Balance Timing (77): Timer begins when movement first stops in a static/motionless state. Applies to Changquan, Jianshu, Daoshu, Qiangshu & Gunshu events only.
  • Forgetting (79): Athlete has a lapse of memory and pauses unconventionally or produces chaotic movements.
