Web between thumb & index finger
01
Energy Gate No. 1 • Lung • Large Intestine

The Hand Gate

Located in the web between the thumb and index finger — a powerful junction on the Large Intestine meridian connecting to the Lung, skin, sinuses, head, and colon. One of the most accessible and effective acupressure points in TCM.

How to stimulate it

With firm pressure from the right thumb, make small circles in the fleshy web of the left hand for 4–5 minutes. Repeat on the right hand.

Supports Mental clarity Sinus congestion Headaches Skin health Colon & digestion Neck & shoulder tension

Center of chest • sternum
02
Energy Gate No. 2 • Heart • All Qi

The Heart Gate

Positioned at the center of the chest on the sternum. This gate governs the entire body’s Qi flow. When it opens, energy moves more freely through every system downstream. The Heart in TCM governs the mind, consciousness, and the quality of sleep.

How to stimulate it

Place one flat palm over the other at the center of the chest. Using light pressure only, circle clockwise at least 200 times. Then reverse for 200 rotations counterclockwise.

Supports Anxiety & worry Depression Heart function Digestive health Mental clarity Qi stagnation
🌀
5 fingers below the navel
03
Energy Gate No. 3 • Kidney • Bladder

The Life Force Gate

Located five finger-widths below the navel — near the Dan Tian, the body’s primary energy reservoir in Taoist practice. This area connects to the Kidney system, which holds the body’s Jing — its foundational essence governing bone health, hormones, and the body’s genetic wisdom.

How to stimulate it

Alternately tap this area with the index and middle fingers of each hand — like gentle drumming. Continue for at least 3 minutes.

Supports Chronic fatigue Low vital energy Hormonal health Kidney & Bladder Immune strength Genetic healing wisdom
⚖
Center dimple of buttocks
04
Energy Gate No. 4 • Gallbladder • Bladder • Liver

The Balance Gate

Located in the center dimple of the main gluteal muscle — where the Gallbladder and Bladder meridians meet. These two channels run head to foot and are responsible for communication between the body’s upper and lower halves. When disconnected, symptoms appear at both ends: headaches above, back and knee pain below.

How to stimulate it

Keep arms, shoulders, and hips relaxed. Make a loose fist and alternately punch this area by swinging slowly from side to side, like a gentle pendulum. Continue for 4–5 minutes.

Supports Lower back pain Hot flashes Menopausal symptoms Liver & Kidney function Hip & sciatic tension Structural alignment
