Dragon's Way Qigong® Carries the Spirit
of Chinese Culture Forward

Chinese civilization has given the world two extraordinary gifts that have never stopped being relevant: Chinese Medicine as a healing art — addressing the whole person across body, mind, and spirit — and Chinese Martial Arts as a path of personal cultivation, demanding discipline, presence, and a sustained relationship with one's own inner power. Dragon's Way Qigong® was created at the intersection of both.

At the heart of Chinese culture is the mystery of the Dragon — not as myth, but as symbol of invisible power. The dragon moves between the visible world and the invisible world. It is unfathomable, timeless and mysterious. That is the spirit of the Dragon this program carries.

Dragon's Way Qigong® was built on one question: What kind of practice truly addresses the relationship between your body, mind, and consciousness? The answer unfolds across every level of the program — from the wisdom of how you eat, all the way to the direct, personal experience of Qi itself.

Not theory. Not temporary relief. A genuine shift in how the body functions, how energy flows, and how life is experienced.

About the Founder

Grand Master Nan Lu —
A Living Tradition

Founder, Dragon’s Way Qigong® & Wu Ming Qigong  ·  TCM World Foundation

Grand Master Nan Lu is a living Taoist master, healer, and teacher — and one of the most rare and authentic voices in traditional Chinese healing alive today. He is the founder of Dragon’s Way Qigong® and the carrier of the Wu Ming Qigong lineage in the West. In a world full of wellness programs and self-proclaimed masters, Grand Master Lu is, simply put, the real deal.

His lineage is not a modern synthesis or a curated wellness approach. Grand Master Lu himself teaches that there are three kinds of Qigong systems in the world — and Wu Ming Qigong belongs to the first: the traditional system, transmitted master to student, generation to generation, traceable all the way back through the lineages. You can follow the chain. It does not begin with him. It runs through him — from a living line that connects back to the ancient Taoist philosophers Lao Tzu and Zhuangzi.

“The first qigong system is a traditional qigong system — a master passes to a student, the student passes to their student, generation to generation. You can trace back to their lineages. Our qigong, Wu Ming Qigong — the lineage is linked with Lao Tzu and Zhuang Tzu.”

— Grand Master Nan Lu

Grand Master Lu founded TCM World Foundation in New York with one purpose: to bring not just the techniques, but the consciousness of Traditional Chinese Medicine to the Western world. He has authored multiple books on Dragon’s Way Qigong and trained hundreds of certified instructors who carry these teachings to students across the United States and beyond.

What distinguishes Grand Master Lu from nearly any other teacher in Qigong or integrative wellness is this: he does not teach techniques alone — he encourages each student to grow. Every class, every program, every conversation he offers is designed to awaken the direct, lived experience of Qi — that invisible yet undeniable life force that ancient Chinese masters understood as the foundation of all health, harmony, and transformation.

When you study Dragon’s Way Qigong®, you are not studying a wellness trend. You are receiving something that has been preserved, passed down, and kept alive across thousands of years — for exactly this moment.
