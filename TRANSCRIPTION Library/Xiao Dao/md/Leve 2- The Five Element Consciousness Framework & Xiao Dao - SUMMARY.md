## Top-level concepts

### The Five Elements as experiential frameworks
- Each element is described as a cluster of: physical sensations, movement patterns, emotional tendencies, typical postural signatures, and musical/tempo affinities.
- Emphasis on experiential diagnosis (what the person reports + how they move) rather than only verbal/medical labels.

### Element summaries (practical highlights)
- Wood: forward momentum, decision-related tension, lateral stretching/mobilization, relevant music: rising/propulsive rhythms.
- Fire: heart-centered openness, social/emotional activation, circular/expansive movements, music: warm, flowing tempos.
- Earth: grounding, digestion-related tension, slow stabilizing movements, music: steady, steady pulses.
- Metal: boundary, clarity, breath-space, refined micro-movements, music: crisp, sparse textures.
- Water: consolidation, rest/recovery, slow lengthening and breath-managed movements, music: slow, ambient / sustained tones.

### Mapping signs to practice choices
- If client shows (sign) X → try (movement) Y with tempo Z and breath cue C.
- Example: shuffling gait + shortened breath (Water/Metal overlap) → lengthening walks with 4-count inhale/6-count exhale + low sustained music.

## Diagnostic algorithm (step-by-step)
1. Intake: ask two concise questions to elicit primary complaint and baseline energy pattern.
2. Movement observation: 60-second walking / standing scan — note asymmetries, breath cadence, head/shoulder alignment.
3. Sensation check: ask for three words describing internal state (e.g., tight, foggy, small, hot).
4. Palpation/mild touch + energetic check (practitioner-guided): note tension clusters and immediate change after a micro-movement cue.
5. Map to element clusters and select 1–2 targeted practices (max 10 minutes) for the session.

## Short practice protocols (copyable)

### 10-minute micro-session — Ground & Reset (Earth primary)
- 2 min: diaphragmatic breathing (4 in / 6 out) standing feet hip-width.
- 4 min: slow hip circles + weighted shifts, music: 60–70 BPM steady pulse.
- 3 min: seated progressive relaxation + guided imagery of roots growing.
- 1 min: tuning question & journaling (What softened?).

### 12-minute activation — Clear & Energize (Wood-Fire blend)
- 2 min: dynamic breath + arm swings.
- 6 min: alternating lunge/step patterns, emphasis on direction changes; music: rising rhythm 90–110 BPM.
- 3 min: chest-open holds + humming for vagal tone.
- 1 min: intention setting / micro-commitment.

## Practitioner cautions & contraindications
- Avoid aggressive stretching or breath holds for clients with uncontrolled hypertension or recent cardiovascular events.
- For trauma-sensitive clients, use grounding, low-intensity practices and obtain consent before hands-on cues.
- For severe dissociation or active psychosis, refer to medical clinician before somatic practices.
